Cyfer Health Monitor

Cyfer Health's Document Terminology

Patient is the person being looked after. Unless you are a professional carer you probably don't think of this person as a 'patient' but instead they are a relative or a friend to whom you are responsible for their welfare.

Carer is the person looking after the patient. This documentation assumes that the reader is the Carer so when 'you' is used, it is referring to the role of the Carer.

There will often be more than one Carer, e.g. siblings looking after a parent; and within a health care organisation there is likely to be a large number of Carers. Likewise, within such a care organisation, there are likely to be a large number of Patients.

Commands and Settings

Cyfer Health Monitor is predominantly an app that monitors the status of patients and hence, once it has been set up, the amount of Carer interaction with the app can be limited. The app's interface consists of a scrolling view of cards each of which provides details about a patient. As new patients are added to the system (i.e. a new patient's Cyfer Health Client app is started) the Monitor's list is updated automatically.

A patient card consists of the patient's photo (if assigned), their short name, the current (or latest) heart reading, how long agao the reading was received and two battery indicators. The first is the battery level of of the patient's device running Cyfer Health Client (typically a phone) and the second is the battery level of the patient's health band. The icons turn red when very low. The health band's icon becomes a symbol when the connection between the patient's device and their health band is lost. This would be typically when the health band has been separated by a few metres.

Configuring Alerts

Alerts are actions that are initiated when a heart rate falls outside of predefined values or if the monitoring connection to a patient is lost. There can be multiple actions assigned to these events. The actions can be:

  1. a notification on the device running Cyfer Health Monitor,
  2. sounding an alarm on that device or
  3. sending an email to a list of predefined email addresses .

On the alerts configuration page, accessed via the three vertical dots menu, there are three sections, one for defining a Warning heart rate event, a Serious heart rate event and a Connection lost event. Actions can be assigned as required to these three sections. It is likely you will need to run the system for a few days to determine what values are usual for your patients, including whether the health device is able to reliably record their heart beats. You may find that a patient takes off the health band, to wash for example, and then often forgets to put it on again. Or, a patient, wears the band too lose to reliably get a reading.

An example configuration:

  • In the Warning section, set low heart rate to 40 and high heart rate to 140. Action #1 is set to send an email to Carers: John, Mary, Jane.
  • In the Serious section, set low heart rate to 35 and high heart rate to 160. Action #1 is set to send an email to Carers: John, Mary, Jane, Sue. Action #2 is to sound the alarm.
  • In the lost Connection section, no actions set.

Finally, you may find it unnecessary to define any actions. Just having the ability to periodically glance at the Monitor to assure yourself everything looks normal may be sufficient. To help with the at-a-glance assessment, the heart rate values of Warning and Serious alerts are also reflected within the Monitor's user interface by changing the colour of the patient cards. If a patient's heart rate is normal (i.e. outside the ranges of Warning and Serious), the card is white. If the patient's heart rate is within the Warning range, it is coloured yellow. If the heart rate is within the Serious range, it is coloured red. If the connection has been lost (i.e. no reading has been for an extended period), the card becomes grey.
