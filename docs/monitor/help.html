Cyfer Health Monitor

Cyfer Health's Document Terminology

Patient is the person being looked after. Unless you are a professional carer you probably don't think of this person as a 'patient' but instead they are a relative or a friend to whom you are responsible for their welfare.

Carer is the person looking after the patient. This documentation assumes that the reader is the Carer so when 'you' is used, it is referring to the role of the Carer.

There will often be more than one Carer, e.g. siblings looking after a parent; and within a health care organisation there is likely to be a large number of Carers. Likewise, within such a care organisation, there are likely to be a large number of Patients.

Commands and Settings

Cyfer Health Monitor is predominantly an app that just monitors the status of patients and hence once it has been set up the amount of Carer interaction with the app can be limited. The app's interface consists of a scrolling view of cards each of which provides details about a patient. As new patients are added to the system (i.e. a new patient's Cyfer Health Client app is started) the Monitor's list is updated automatically.

Commands available from the three vertical dots menu

Magnifying glass with minus icon Reduces the size of the patient cards, allowing more cards to be viewed without the need to scroll.
Magnifying glass with plus icon Increases the size of the patient cards, providing easier viewing of each patients information however the Carere will need to scroll through the lsit to view all the patients' cards.
Alerts Configure various actions that will take place when an unusually low or high high rate is received.
Hide/show Toggles hiding or showing individual patients.
Charts on/off Toggles showing the charts.
Settings Configure various settings that are required for Cyfer Health Monitor to function.
Log A debugging command. This shouldn't be necessary to use.
Simulate on/off Just used to demo Cyfer Health. It creates artificial patients and artificial heart rate readings.
